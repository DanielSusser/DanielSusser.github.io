Size matters. Sample size that is. We want to know that the results we get are reliable - whether it's changing the size of the donate button on a charity website, or giving some patients a new treatment, we need to know that what we do really makes a difference.

When you run an experiment, you are trying to work out the size of an effect. This baseline can be a control group, experiencing the same experiment, or a result from an existing data set that you are trying to beat.

Large experiments are able to detect small effects, but it's hard to know how many people you need to make sure your experiment is 'statistically significant'. For this, SureFyr can help you calculate your sample size.

Chance is a funny thing, and even if you have tonnes of data, it's still possible that your result is a statistical fluke. Confidence level refers to the likelihood that yours results are down to chance. A result with 95% confidence means that there is a 5% chance that your hypothesis isn't true, but your results look good anyway. 95% would need a larger sample size than a 90% confidence result.

If your sample size is large enough to detect the effect size in your experiment with an acceptable degree of confidence, we say your experiment is powered to detect your effect.

The larger the sample size, the more confident you can be in your results. SureFyr is a user friendly power calculator that helps you to work out how many people you need in your test, or it can tell you if a test you have already run can give you reliable results.
